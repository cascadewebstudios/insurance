What is Property Mortgage Insurance?

Property mortgage insurance is a type of insurance designed to protect lenders by reducing the risk of borrower default on a mortgage loan. It is commonly required in Canada when a homebuyer makes a down payment of less than 20% of the property value, making homeownership more accessible to a wider range of people.

This insurance acts as a financial safeguard for lenders, allowing borrowers to secure mortgages with smaller upfront payments. While it primarily protects the lender’s interests, it also supports the overall stability of the housing market by enabling more Canadians to purchase homes.

Importance
Mortgage insurance plays a critical role in Canada’s real estate market by protecting lenders against loss, helping reduce risk associated with smaller down payments, and expanding access to homeownership. Understanding how it works and its implications can help borrowers make informed decisions when financing their homes.
COVERAGE INFORMATION

What Does Property Mortgage Insurance Cover?

  • Protection Against Default: Mortgage insurance protects lenders by covering losses if a borrower is unable to meet mortgage payments.
  • Required for High-Ratio Mortgages: Typically mandatory when the down payment is less than 20% of the property value, enabling buyers to enter the market sooner.
  • Coverage Linked to Mortgage Balance: The amount covered generally corresponds to the outstanding mortgage balance and may decrease over time as payments are made.
  • Premiums Paid by Borrower: Borrowers pay premiums either upfront or added to their mortgage payments, which can vary depending on loan size and risk factors.
  • Coverage Duration: Mortgage insurance typically remains in place until the mortgage balance falls below a certain threshold, often 80% of the home's value.
  • Does Not Protect Borrower Directly: This insurance primarily protects the lender, not the homeowner’s personal finances.
  • Not a Substitute for Home Insurance: Mortgage insurance does not cover property damage or liability; homeowners must maintain separate home insurance policies.
  • Coverage Limits and Exclusions: Specific terms vary by provider, so borrowers should carefully review policy details and exclusions.
  • No Coverage for Voluntary Defaults: Failure to pay the mortgage by choice is not covered.
CRITICAL COMPONENTS

Mortgage Insurance Criteria & Considerations

Down Payment Threshold

Mortgage insurance is generally required for homebuyers with a down payment of less than 20% of the purchase price.

Mortgage Terms and Conditions

Coverage terms, premiums, and duration vary by policy and provider. It is important to review these details carefully.

Premium Costs

Premiums are typically based on the size of the mortgage, the risk profile of the borrower, and the loan-to-value ratio.

Lender and Provider Requirements

Many lenders require mortgage insurance for high-ratio mortgages and may work with government-backed or private insurers.

Market Stability

Mortgage insurance contributes to the stability of the housing market by mitigating lender risk and supporting responsible lending practices.

Informed Decision-Making

Borrowers should compare policies, understand exclusions, and assess affordability before selecting mortgage insurance.
